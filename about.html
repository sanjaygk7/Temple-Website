About Kudtheri Shree Mahamaya Temple

The Shree Mahamaya Temple is one of the oldest temples in the city and worships Goddess Shakti. History says that the idol of Goddess Mahamaya installed in the Temple was brought from Kudtheri village in Goa in the 16th century by the GSB community. It was owing to this reason that the temple was set up close by the Sri Venkatramana Temple at Car street.

The tank in front of the temple called Shri Mahamaya Tirtha, is the largest tank in the city. It attracts crowds of devotees to witness the immersion of clay idols of Ganesha during Ganesh Chathurthi & Sharada during Navratri Festival.

The famous Shri Venkatramana temple is only 100 Yards away from Shri Mahamaya temple. Both the temples are like two eyes of the GSB community. They work in total harmony, mutual understanding & Co-operation. Food is served for GSB community people everyday.

About Bantwal Kashi Math

Kudtheri Mahamaya temple is one of the oldest temples of Mangalore. Goddess Mahamaya installed here was brought from Kudtheri village in Goa in the 16th century by the GSB community who fled Goa due to Portuguese atrocities. This temple also has idols of Srinivasa, Venkatramana, Mahalaxmi, Ganapati, Raktheshwari, Chandramauleeshwar & Mahalasa Narayani. Mahamaya Tirtha just in front of the temple is the largest tank in Mangalore. The popular festivals associated with this temple are the Car Festival & Navratri. During Ganesh Chathurthi & Navratri festival, clay idols of Ganapati & Sharada are immersed in the Mahamaya tank after night long colourful processions.
